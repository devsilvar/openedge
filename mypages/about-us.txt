About Us Page
A different kind of hospitality firm built for owners who want their businesses to work and

Open Edge is a hospitality solutions firm that fixes the system and middle-management

We help hospitality owners build real wealth and a lasting legacy from their investments

We exist because too many hospitality businesses in Nigeria stay busy, but never truly work.
Why Open Edge Exists
Most hospitality failures are not caused by poor intentions or lack of effort.
They happen because:
• Owners are forced to stay deeply involved in daily operations
• Middle management lacks structure, accountability, and growth paths
• Systems are weak, undocumented, or ignored
• Decisions are made under pressure, without visibility
The result is a business that survives but cannot scale, stabilize, or endure.
Open Edge was created to address this gap practically, not theoretically.
The Open Edge Operating System
At the core of our work is the Open Edge Operating System. A structured way of running 
hospitality businesses that reflects the realities of the Nigerian market.
It accounts for:
• Nigerian operating environments and constraints
• Owners’ lifestyle and return expectations
• Staff turnover and skill gaps
• Human behaviour under pressure
• Owner fatigue and decision overload
This is not imported hospitality jargon.
It is not theory.
It is a practical operating system, developed and refined inside real hospitality businesses.

Why Early Clients Trust Us
Early clients didn’t come to Open Edge because we were loud.
They came because we were clear, structured, and accountable.
Here’s what consistently earns their trust:
1) We start with assessment, not assumptions
This prevents expensive guesswork and ensures every intervention is deliberate and 
relevant.
2) We work inside the business, not around it
We sit with owners, management, and staff to understand how work actually gets 
done, then redesign what is broken.
3) We focus on root causes, not symptoms
Most hospitality problems show up as poor staff performance, revenue leakage, and 
owner exhaustion, but we trace these back to system and middle-management

4) We respect the owner’s reality
